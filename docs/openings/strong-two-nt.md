# Strong 2NT Opening

### Opening Description

- **Strength**: 21-22 HCP
- **Suit Distribution**: Balanced distribution

??? note "Response Structure"
    ### Response Structure

    | Response | Meaning                                                    |
    | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
    | 3♣       | Stayman                                                    |
    | 3♦       | Transfer to ♥                                              |
    | 3♥       | Transfer to ♠                                              |
    | 3♠       | Minor preference; 5-4+ in minors with a major singleton    |
    | 3NT      | Natural, to play                                           |
    | 4♣/4♦    | Natural, forcing                                           |
    | 4♥/4♠    | Natural, 6+, to play                                       |
    | 4NT      | Quantitative slam invitation; balanced, no four-card major |

    #### Notes
    - With slam interest and a six-card major, use the 3♦/3♥ transfer rather than a direct 4♥/4♠.
    - With a worthless hand and only a five-card major, pass rather than transferring.
    - With a six-card major and likely 3+ card support from opener, bid game.

??? note "2NT – 3♦/3♥ (Transfers)"
    ### 2NT – 3♦/3♥ (Transfers)

    After a transfer (3♦ → ♥, 3♥ → ♠), opener shows the quality of support and control structure:

    | Opener's Bid  | Meaning                                                                    |
    | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
    | 3♥ / 3♠       | Accepts the transfer with doubleton support                                |
    | 3NT           | Three-card support                                                         |
    | 4♥ / 4♠       | Four-card support, but not control-rich                                    |
    | 3♠ / 4♣ / 4♦  | Side suit + four-card support (or strong three-card support), control-rich |

    #### Notes
    - Control-rich implies suitable controls for slam exploration.
    - Side suit bids show a real suit and help evaluate slam prospects.
    - With a six-card major and 3+ card support from opener, responder bids game.
    - With a worthless hand and a five-card major, responder passes rather than transferring.
    - With a five-card major, responder bids only when combined strength and a probable fit suggest game values.

??? note "2NT – 3♠ (Minor-Suit Sign-Off)"
    ### 2NT – 3♠ (Minor-Suit Sign-Off)

    - Shows 5-4+ in the minor suits with a major singleton.
    - With good minor-suit fits, opener should avoid 3NT.

??? note "2NT – 4NT (Quantitative)"
    ### 2NT – 4NT (Quantitative)

    - Balanced hand, no four-card major.
    - Invites opener to bid a small slam.
